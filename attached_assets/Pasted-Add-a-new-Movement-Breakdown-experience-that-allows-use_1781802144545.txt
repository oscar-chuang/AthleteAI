Add a new “Movement Breakdown” experience that allows users to visually see exactly what the AI is analyzing, instead of only reading a report afterward.

Goal:
Make the analysis more transparent, interactive, educational, and engaging. Users should be able to see how the AI reached its conclusions.

Movement Breakdown Experience:

After analysis is complete, create a dedicated section called:

“Movement Breakdown”

or

“Watch Your Analysis”

This section should display the original video with the pose skeleton rendered directly on top of the athlete throughout the entire movement.

Video Playback Features:

* Display the full skeleton overlay in real time.
* Show tracked joints and body segments clearly.
* Allow users to pause, rewind, fast-forward, and scrub through the timeline.
* Maintain smooth playback performance.

AI Coaching Moments:

As the video plays, the AI should identify important moments and place markers along the timeline.

Examples:

* Good hip extension
* Knee collapse detected
* Balance loss
* Excessive forward lean
* Strong landing position
* Shoulder alignment issue

When playback reaches one of these moments:

* Automatically pause the video.
* Highlight the relevant body area.
* Dim the rest of the screen slightly.
* Display a simple coaching card.

The coaching card should contain:

Title:
“What We Noticed”

Explanation:
A short, easy-to-understand observation.

Why It Matters:
A simple explanation of performance implications.

Suggested Fix:
One actionable correction.

Buttons:

* Continue Playback
* Replay Moment
* Learn More

Visual Highlighting:

When an issue is detected:

* Highlight affected joints or limbs.
* Draw arrows showing movement direction when relevant.
* Display measured angles when useful.
* Use color coding:
    * Green = strong movement
    * Yellow = improvement opportunity
    * Red = significant issue

Keep visuals clean and understandable.

Timeline Markers:

Add a timeline below the video showing:

* Key strengths
* Technique issues
* Risk flags
* Coaching moments

Users should be able to tap any marker and instantly jump to that moment.

End-of-Video Summary:

After playback finishes, present a comprehensive movement review.

Title:
“Your Movement Summary”

Include:

Movement Flow Score
Evaluate how smoothly the athlete moved from start to finish.

Movement Efficiency
Assess whether energy was transferred efficiently throughout the movement.

Body Control
Evaluate stability, balance, and coordination.

Consistency
Evaluate whether movement patterns remained consistent throughout the exercise.

Rhythm and Timing
Evaluate sequencing and tempo when relevant.

Strengths
Top 3 strengths observed.

Improvement Opportunities
Top 3 areas for improvement.

Most Important Fix
Identify the single highest-impact correction.

Coach’s Summary

Provide a concise explanation of:

* How the movement looked overall
* Where energy was lost
* Where mechanics broke down
* What should be prioritized next

Advanced Requirements:

Only generate observations supported by actual tracked data, measured angles, pose landmarks, or visual evidence.

Do not invent issues.

If confidence is low:

* Display uncertainty.
* Explain why confidence is limited.

Each observation should include:

* Confidence score
* Supporting evidence
* Relevant frame or timestamp

User Experience Goals:

The user should feel like they are reviewing game film with a high-level coach.

The experience should be:

* Interactive
* Visual
* Easy to understand
* Educational
* Trustworthy
* Engaging enough that users want to watch the full breakdown rather than skip directly to the report.

The final result should feel closer to Hudl, Overtime Elite film review, or professional sports analysis software than a traditional AI text report.
