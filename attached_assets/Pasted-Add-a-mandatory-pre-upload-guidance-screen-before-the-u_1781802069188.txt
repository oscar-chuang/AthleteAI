Add a mandatory pre-upload guidance screen before the user selects or uploads a video.

Goal:
Improve video quality and analysis accuracy by clearly teaching users how to record a usable video before they upload.

Requirements:

Create a polished, visually appealing “Recording Tips” screen that appears before every video upload. The screen should explain that AthleteAI performs best when the athlete is clearly visible and fully in frame.

The screen should include:

Title:
“Get the Most Accurate Analysis”

Subtitle:
“Follow these tips before recording or uploading your video.”

Best Practices Section:
✓ Keep your entire body visible from head to toe throughout the movement.
✓ Make sure all arms and legs remain in frame at all times.
✓ Record from a reasonable distance so your full body is visible.
✓ Use good lighting.
✓ Keep the camera steady.
✓ Avoid crowded backgrounds.
✓ Record from an angle that clearly shows the movement.
✓ Make sure the athlete is the primary subject in the video.

Common Mistakes Section:
✗ Standing too far away.
✗ Cropping feet, hands, or parts of the body out of the frame.
✗ Poor lighting.
✗ Shaky video.
✗ Multiple athletes visible.
✗ Recording from an angle that blocks key movements.

Visual Examples:
Add simple visual illustrations or placeholder image cards showing:

* Good example: athlete fully visible.
* Bad example: athlete too far away.
* Bad example: athlete partially cropped.
* Bad example: poor lighting.

User Acknowledgement:
Add a checkbox:
“I understand these recording guidelines.”

The Continue button should remain disabled until the checkbox is selected.

Optional Enhancement:
Add a “Don’t show this again” toggle and save the preference locally.

Future-Ready Enhancement:
Structure the screen so it can later support automated video quality checks such as:

* Full body visibility detection.
* Lighting quality checks.
* Camera distance estimation.
* Occlusion detection.
* Multiple-person detection.

Design Requirements:
Make the page feel premium and modern.
Use clear icons, illustrations, spacing, and color.
Avoid large blocks of text.
Optimize for mobile devices.
Make the guidance easy for a middle school athlete, parent, or coach to understand within 10 seconds.

Success Criteria:
A first-time user should immediately understand:

1. The athlete must be fully visible.
2. All limbs should remain in frame.
3. The camera should not be too far away.
4. Better video quality leads to more accurate analysis.
